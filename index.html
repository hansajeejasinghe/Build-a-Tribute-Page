C. W. W. Kannangara

The man who made Education free for all


Here's a time line of C. W. W. Kannangara's life:

  • 1884 - C.W.W. Kannangara is born in the small village of Randombe in present-day Galle District, Sri Lanka.
  • 1905 - Kannangara graduates from Richmond College, Galle, and begins his career as a teacher.
  • 1915 - Kannangara earns a degree from the University of London and returns to Sri Lanka to continue his career in education.
  • 1931 - Kannangara is appointed Minister of Education in the State Council, the governing body of British Ceylon.
  • 1940 - Kannangara introduces the Free Education Bill, which provides for free education for all Sri Lankan children up to the age of 14.
  • 1945 - The Free Education Bill is passed into law, marking a significant milestone in Sri Lanka's education system.
  • 1947 - Kannangara leads efforts to promote the use of the Sinhala language in education, a move that helps to establish a stronger sense of national identity in Sri Lanka.
  • 1950 - Kannangara is awarded the national honour of "Deshabandu" in recognition of his contributions to education.
  • 1953 - Kannangara passes away in Colombo, Sri Lanka, at the age of 69.
  • 1978 - Kannangara is posthumously awarded the national honour of "Deshamanya" in recognition of his contributions to education and public service.
  • Today - Kannangara is remembered as a national hero in Sri Lanka and his portrait adorns Sri Lankan currency notes, stamps, and public buildings.
